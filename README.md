# Caden-Torosian-Stock-Analysis
This repository holds a very powerful Streamlit application. It can be used to find stocks based on a variety of criteria, evaluate stocks based on 7 different models for short and long term trades, as well as compare stocks, deep-dive research, and more. The possibilities are endless and available for free.

Torosian Stock Insights is a full-stack equity research platform built in Python and Streamlit, designed to give retail investors access to institutional-grade analysis tools in one unified interface. The platform pulls live market data from Yahoo Finance and covers the entire S&P Composite 1500 — approximately 1,500 large, mid, and small cap stocks drawn dynamically from the S&P 500, S&P 400, and S&P 600 indices.
The platform is organized across three screens. The Learn page serves as an interactive reference guide documenting all 26 technical indicators used throughout the app, including plain-English explanations, buy and sell signal conditions, and difficulty ratings for each. It also explains the platform's sector-adjusted threshold system, which calibrates oversold and overbought levels differently for each of seven sectors — recognizing, for example, that a Technology stock at RSI 71 is normal market behavior while the same reading in Real Estate signals genuine overextension.
The Screener and Analysis screen is the core of the platform. The Screener tab lets users filter the full 1,500-stock universe by sector, market cap tier, risk tolerance, time horizon, and minimum technical score, with an advanced filter layer that allows indicator-specific screening for bullish or bearish signals and single-indicator sorting. The Deep Dive tab provides a full single-stock breakdown including a composite technical score gauge, one-year price chart with moving averages, all 26 indicator readings, and key fundamental metrics. The Compare tab enables head-to-head analysis of any two stocks with normalized performance charts, fundamental comparison tables, and a breakdown of exactly which indicators the two stocks disagree on.
The Financial Modeling screen houses seven professional-grade analytical models organized into three categories. The Trend Models include Stan Weinstein's Stage Analysis using 40-week moving averages on weekly data, Mansfield Relative Strength comparing any stock against the S&P 500, and a Mean Reversion model combining Bollinger Bands with RSI to identify stretched trades. The Psychological Models include Elliott Wave analysis with scipy-powered peak and trough detection overlaid with Fibonacci retracement levels, and a Volume Profile with Anchored VWAP showing price levels where the most shares have traded alongside the Point of Control and Value Area. The Value Models include a Discounted Cash Flow calculator with adjustable WACC, growth rate, terminal growth, and projection horizon inputs pulled from live financial statements, and a CAN SLIM ranking system that scores stocks across all seven of William O'Neil's criteria using live earnings data and institutional ownership from 13F filings.
